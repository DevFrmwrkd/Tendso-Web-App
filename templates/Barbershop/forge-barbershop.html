The shop

Built around
the chair

Forge started as a two-chair shop a block off the Williamsburg waterfront, run by barbers who were tired of rushed, assembly-line haircuts.

Take your time, get it right, send people back out feeling like themselves.

A decade later the room is bigger but the rule hasn't changed. Every barber on the floor is state-licensed and trained in-house — and we sharpen our own blades, hone our own straight razors, and keep the towels hot.

We're a neighborhood shop first — the kind of place where regulars get the same chair, and first-timers leave as regulars.

— The Forge barbers

Good to know

FAQ

Still wondering about something? Message the shop — we'll get straight back to you.

Do I need an appointment?

No — walk-ins are always welcome whenever a chair's free. If you'd rather guarantee a time with your usual barber, you can book a chair online in under a minute.

What are your opening hours?

We're open seven days a week. Live, current hours are shown on our Google Business listing and at the bottom of this page.

Do you cut all hair types?

Yes. Our barbers are trained across straight, wavy, curly and coily hair, and across clipper, scissor and razor work.

Can you cut kids' hair?

Absolutely — "The Little Gents" is a patient, no-pressure cut made for younger clients, including nervous first-timers.

How do I pay?

We take cards, contactless and cash in the shop. Booking online doesn't require payment up front — you settle after your cut.

Where exactly are you and is there parking?

We're on Wythe Avenue in Williamsburg, a short walk from the Bedford Av subway. Metered street parking is usually available nearby — see the map below.
