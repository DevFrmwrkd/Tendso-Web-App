# Taste Ledger

Append-only. Every entry dated. Every entry has a mandatory `why:`.
Read this before generating. Append any new judgement calls after.

---

## LIKE ✅

- **2026-05-19** — Distinct character per bucket, not template recolours. *why: nine pages that share a block set should still feel like nine different businesses; sameness is the failure mode of "template families".*
- **2026-05-19** — Finished, real sample content on first load. *why: the operator decides what to keep by deletion; that only works if every block ships populated and convincing.*
- **2026-05-19** — Deselect model over compose model. *why: customising by deletion is faster and lower-risk than picking blocks from a menu; it also forces the superset to be coherent.*
- **2026-05-19** — Designer owns the visuals (layout, type, hierarchy, motion). *why: design direction by committee produces averages; one strong point of view per page produces character.*
- **2026-05-19** — Editorial/print sensibility on at least some pages (heavy type, generous space, asymmetric grids). *why: "small-business website" defaults to a tired SaaS pattern; print discipline is a cheap way to break it.*
- **2026-05-19** — Intentionally designed image placeholders that read as part of the composition (framed, captioned, tinted, gridded). *why: a designed slot communicates "swap your photo here"; a grey box communicates "we forgot".*
- **2026-05-19** — Trust signals integrated into design, not crammed into a logo strip. *why: licences, years-in-business and credentials are the actual product on YMYL pages; treat them as content, not chrome.*
- **2026-05-19** — Monospace + serif/display mixes for editorial blocks; one strong type voice per page. *why: a single, committed type system is the cheapest way to make a page feel designed rather than assembled.*
- **2026-05-19** — Service area as a real map or list of named places, not a vague "we serve the area" line. *why: the buyer is checking whether you cover their street; specificity is the conversion lever.*
- **2026-05-19** — FAQs written like answers a human would give, not SEO bait. *why: FAQs are read after the hero — if they sound like keyword stuffing the rest of the page evaporates with them.*

## AVOID ❌

- **2026-05-19** — Run-on `Name: <long description> in City` headlines. *why: this is the exact failure pattern this brief exists to fix; SEO lives in title/description/schema, not in the visible H1.*
- **2026-05-19** — Pages with no imagery. *why: a stranger should believe this is a live business; no images breaks that within two seconds.*
- **2026-05-19** — Visible `{{TOKENS}}`, `Lorem ipsum`, or `Business Name Here`. *why: anything visibly unfinished tells the visitor they're looking at a draft, not a business.*
- **2026-05-19** — Hardcoded prices, promos, discounts, regular staff names, or year/date references that go stale. *why: these are operator-edited and Google-Business-Profile-bound; if you hardcode them the page rots silently.*
- **2026-05-19** — Over-prescribed look that reads as a template (identical hero geometry, identical "feature card" grid, identical pastel CTA). *why: if all nine pages look like the same Webflow theme the deselect model collapses.*
- **2026-05-19** — JS-required content. *why: the page must crawl and read with JS off; JS is a re-renderer, not a renderer.*
- **2026-05-19** — Emoji as a primary visual device. *why: cheap, dated, breaks the design voice; reach for real type, real photos, real shapes instead.*
- **2026-05-19** — Gradient-pill CTAs floating over a hero with soft drop-shadows. *why: 2018 SaaS-landing-page cliché; a button can be a flat rectangle of confident type and still convert.*
- **2026-05-19** — Hand-drawn SVG illustrations meant to look like photos or icons of people. *why: model-generated SVGs always look uncanny; use real photos or honest geometric placeholders.*
- **2026-05-19** — Three-feature-card grid (icon + heading + sentence × 3) used as "why choose us". *why: the most predictable block on the internet; if you can't avoid the structure, at least avoid the icon-circle.*
- **2026-05-19** — Fake review counts or star ratings hardcoded into markup. *why: GBP-bound; faking it is a legal and credibility risk. Leave the slot, comment the source.*
