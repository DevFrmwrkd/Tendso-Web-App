About the kitchen

A kitchen, not a concept.

Pico & Olive started as a small bakery counter on St James's Street, run out of a converted laundry by two cooks who'd worked together on a tiny island in the Aegean. The wood oven came first; the dining room grew around it.

The food is what we feed ourselves: a handful of mezze, a flatbread we change with the season, lamb when it's good, a fish when it's better. We bake bread every morning at five and put the leftovers in the soup.

It's a kitchen, not a concept. We aren't trying to be anywhere else. We're trying to be honest about Tuesday.

Why come back

Three things we never compromise.

  1. 01

    We bake every loaf on site.

    Sourdough starter from 2014, mixed by hand, baked in the same oven we cook on. Nothing arrives in a bag.

  2. 02

    Short menu, slow turnover.

    We cook six mezze and three flatbreads a day. When something runs out we write something new on the wall.

  3. 03

    Suppliers we can name.

    Sussex flour from Stoneham Bakehouse, olive oil from a co-op in Mani, fish from Newhaven that morning.

Frequently asked

Before you walk in.

If we haven't answered it here, ring the kitchen on 01273 224 901 or drop us a line.

Do you take bookings?

Evening tables only, Tuesday to Saturday. Breakfast and lunch are walk-in. Reserve below, by phone, or by message.

Are you vegetarian-friendly?

Most of the menu is vegetarian. Vegan flatbreads and mezze are clearly marked. We can adapt almost anything.

Do you have gluten-free bread?

We can swap our flatbread for a buckwheat seed cracker. We bake in a flour-heavy kitchen, so it isn't a coeliac-safe environment.

Is the room accessible?

Step-free entry from the side door on Madeira Place. The accessible WC is on the ground floor. Please ring ahead so we can hold a table near the door.

Can we bring children?

Yes — until 7pm. We have two highchairs and a quieter corner table. Ask when you book.

Do you do private events?

We can take the whole room from Sunday lunch onwards. Twelve to twenty-two guests, one set menu, one long table.

Is there parking?

Pay-and-display on Madeira Place; the Royal Sussex Hospital car park is two minutes' walk.
