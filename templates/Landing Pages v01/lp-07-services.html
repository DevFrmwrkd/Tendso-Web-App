About the shop

Two tailors. Thirty-six years of bench-work.

Northline opened in 2011 above what was then a vinyl shop on Broughton Street. The vinyl shop is gone; we got the ground floor in 2015 and the workroom in the back grew enough to fit two benches, a Hoffman press, and the espresso machine that everyone judged us for.

Between the two of us we have thirty-six years on the bench — Edinburgh, Glasgow, and ten months in Naples for one of us. We trained in cutting and finishing both, which is unusual now and means we don't outsource anything: if you bring it in, the person looking at you is the person sewing it.

We don't dry-clean. We don't sell anything off-the-peg. We do invisible mending and we will keep mending a coat for as long as you want to keep it. The longest active mend on our books is from 2013.

Why us

Three things we commit to.

i.

Cutter and finisher in-house.

Every garment passes through one person's hands from chalk mark to last stitch. Nothing is sent out.

ii.

House cloths and yours.

Holland & Sherry, Vitale Barberis, Drago, Fox. Bring your own cloth and we'll receipt it.

iii.

Three-fitting guarantee.

On any made-to-measure work — pattern fit, full baste, finishing. Adjustments included for a year.

Questions

Before you
bring it in.

If yours isn't here, telephone the shop on 0131 558 7421.

How long does an alteration take?

Most jobs are one week. We hold a small number of 48-hour rush slots; ask when you book.

Do you take walk-ins?

For drop-off and collection, yes. For fittings please book — we keep the shop calm and won't have you waiting.

Can I send something in?

Yes. Use our 'send by post' form to print a label and instructions. We acknowledge receipt within a working day.

Do you alter wedding dresses?

Yes, up to three fittings included. Book at least six weeks before the date if you can.

Will you alter something I bought online?

Yes. Whatever you bring in, we'll quote fairly. If it can't be made to fit, we'll say so before we cut a stitch.

Do you do leather?

Light leather — yes. Suedes and heavy leather we send to a specialist with whom we share a workshop.

Are you wheelchair accessible?

Step-free entry from Broughton Place. The fitting room is large enough for a wheelchair turn.
