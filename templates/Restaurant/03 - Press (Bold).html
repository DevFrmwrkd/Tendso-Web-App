The house

A stall, a grill,
and a long obsession
with the market.

Sorrel began as a market stall and a borrowed grill. It is now a small dining room where the menu is written each morning around what the farms send in.

Almost everything passes through oak and almond wood — a flavour no dial can imitate. Our bread is milled in-house, vegetables arrive twice weekly from growers we know by name, and we cook the whole animal so nothing is wasted.

— Mara & the Sorrel kitchen

Why Sorrel

Three things we keep sacred.

The Hearth

A single wood fire is the heart of the kitchen. No gas line, no shortcuts — only oak, almond, and patience.

The Market

The menu is rewritten each morning around what local growers actually pick — never the other way around.

Good to know

Questions

Do you take walk-ins?+
Yes — the bar and a handful of counter seats are kept for walk-ins every night. The dining room books up, so reserve ahead for a table at a set time.
Allergies & dietary needs?+
Always. Tell us when you book or sit down. Because we cook to the market we can flex most dishes, with strong vegetable and gluten-free options every night.
Is there a tasting menu?+
Yes — the Hearth Menu is a chef's selection of the day's best, served family-style. Book the counter to watch it come together.
Do you do corkage?+
We do, for a per-bottle fee, on bottles not already on our list. Share a taste with the team and it's on us.
Where should I park?+
Street parking around Hayes Valley plus the Civic Center garage two blocks east. A short walk from Van Ness Muni, and a bike rack out front.
