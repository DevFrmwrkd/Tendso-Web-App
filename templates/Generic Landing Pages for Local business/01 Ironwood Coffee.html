Our story

A corner roastery, built on the long pull.

Ironwood started with a borrowed drum roaster and a stubborn belief that a neighborhood deserves coffee roasted close enough to smell it on the walk in.

# Pour by pour, since 2014.

Today we still roast in the front window, weigh every dose, and dial each batch the morning it's poured. Nothing sits on a shelf for a month.

We buy from a small circle of growers we've kept for years, pay above market, and print the harvest date on every bag — because freshness you can't taste isn't a claim worth making.

Good to know

Questions, answered.

How fresh is the coffee, really?+
We roast three mornings a week and most beans are poured or bagged within a day or two of roasting. Every retail bag is stamped with its roast date so you never have to guess.
Do you grind beans for me?+
Yes — tell us your brewer at the counter (or note it on a subscription) and we'll grind to match, from espresso fine to French-press coarse. We always recommend whole bean if you have a grinder at home.
Can I set up a wholesale account?+
We work with a handful of nearby restaurants and offices on standing weekly deliveries, including espresso dial-in support and barista training. Reach out through the contact button and we'll talk through volume.
Is there seating, and is it laptop-friendly?+
There's a counter, a few stools, and a bench by the window. Mornings get busy with regulars; afternoons are quieter if you're settling in for a while.
