# Creator Marketing Playbook — Tendso

> Knowledge base for **Certified Creators** (field agents). Read this end-to-end before your first pitch. Every number, fee, and timeline below is taken directly from how the app actually works — don't quote anything not in here.

---

## 1. What Tendso is

**One-sentence pitch:** We turn small Filipino businesses into real, on-the-internet businesses — and we pay creators like you to make it happen.

**The product:**
- A creator (you) visits a local business, takes a few photos, records a short interview with the owner, and submits everything through the mobile app.
- Our AI pipeline turns that into a clean, mobile-ready **website** for the business — with their products, photos, contact info, and story.
- We handle hosting, deployment, and (optionally) the custom domain.
- You earn cash for every successful submission.

**Why this exists:** Most Philippine MSMEs (micro, small, and medium enterprises) don't have a website. They live on Facebook pages, GCash QR codes, and Viber chats. When a customer searches "candle maker Quezon City" on Google, they don't show up. We fix that — fast and cheap — and creators get paid to bring their kuya/ate's tindahan online.

**Tagline (from the landing page):** *"Digitize MSMEs. Get Paid."*

## 2. Who the customer is

The **business owner** — the kuya, ate, tito, tita, or family running:

- Sari-sari stores expanding into a small product line
- Home-based food businesses (lechon manok, kakanin, pastries, milk tea)
- Service providers (laundry, salon, barbershop, repair shops, photographers)
- Craft / product makers (candles, soaps, accessories, fashion)
- Single-location shops without an in-house marketing person

**They are NOT:**
- Businesses that already have a working website + e-commerce
- Multi-branch chains (they have agencies)
- Anyone uncomfortable being on camera (offer audio-only — see §6)

**What they care about (in order):**
1. Cost — every peso matters at this size.
2. Time — they're already running the business solo or with family.
3. Looking professional to customers and suppliers.
4. Getting found on Google / messengers without learning tech.

## 3. The pitch — what to actually say

Three lines. Memorize this and adapt the wording:

> *"Po, may pang-business kayo na website na po ba kayo? Hindi naman po Facebook page — yung sariling .com address na pwedeng hanapin sa Google. Kami po sa Tendso, gumagawa kami ng professional website para sa local businesses tulad niyo. ₱1,000 lang po — isang bayad, hindi monthly. Kasama na ang website, hosting, at lahat ng kakailanganin."*

Then if they're interested:

> *"Pwede ko po kayong tanungin ng konti tungkol sa business niyo? Mga 5 minutes lang po. Tatanungin ko po yung pangalan, ano ginagawa, mga products, kanino niyo binebenta. Tapos magku-kuha lang po ako ng 3 photos: yung storefront, isang shot ng products, at isa sa inyo. Yan na po. Within 24-48 hours, may mga website na po kayo."*

Key principles:
- **Lead with the price**, not the features. ₱1,000 is the magic number for MSMEs.
- **One-time payment**, no monthly. This is the single biggest objection-killer vs. competitors.
- **Filipino-first language.** Code-switch only if they speak English first.
- **5 minutes** — be honest. The interview is short. They have a business to run.
- **Don't oversell the AI.** Just say "professional website." Owners don't care how it's made.

## 4. Pricing — exactly what to quote

Two tiers. **Pick one with the owner during the interview**, then mark it on the app.

| Package | Price (one-time, paid by business owner) | What's included |
|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | **₱1,000** | Professional website on a free Tendso subdomain (e.g. `juansbakery.tendso.com`) |
| **With Custom Domain** | **₱1,500** | Everything above + their own domain (`juansbakery.com`) for **year 1**, fully owned by them |

**Critical talking points on the custom domain tier:**

- The **₱1,500 includes the first year of the domain**. The business owner pays no extra for that year.
- After year 1, **the domain is theirs**. We do **NOT** auto-renew. Renewal is approximately ₱1,120/year (~$20). The owner controls when and whether to renew. If they don't renew, the domain just expires — no surprise charges, no contract.
- We send them a **reminder email 30 days before expiry**.
- Available TLDs: `.com`, `.net`, `.shop`, `.store`, `.online`, `.xyz`, `.co`, `.site`. **No `.ph`** — too expensive for our included budget.

**When to pitch which tier:**

| Situation | Recommend |
|---|---|
| First-time business going online, just starting | Standard ₱1,000 |
| Already on Facebook with a brand name they care about | Custom Domain ₱1,500 |
| Business with a name that's hard to spell | Custom Domain (it shortens it on cards/menus) |
| Owner already owns a domain they want to use | Standard — we'll connect their existing domain post-launch |
| Pure budget play, the ₱500 difference matters | Standard ₱1,000 |

**Don't recommend the higher tier just for a bigger creator earning** — your earning is the same either way (see §5). Match the tier to what the owner actually needs.

## 5. Your earnings — per submission

The creator's payout depends on the interview format you capture. **Same payout regardless of which package the owner picks** (standard vs. custom domain).

| Format | You earn | What's required |
|---|---|---|
| **Video Interview** | **₱500** | 3+ photos + a video interview |
| **Audio Only** | **₱300** | 3+ photos + an audio interview |

**When to record video vs. audio:**

- **Default to video.** Looks better on the website, pays you more, the AI gets more context (body language, environment).
- **Switch to audio if** the owner is camera-shy, the location is loud / poorly-lit / cramped, or they explicitly ask. Don't force a bad video.
- **Recording length:** 1–3 minutes is plenty. Quality > duration. The AI extracts story details, not transcripts. Keep them comfortable.

**You're paid when:**
1. Submission is approved by our team (24–48 hours).
2. Business owner pays the ₱1,000 / ₱1,500 service fee.
3. Website goes live.

**Withdrawal flow** (Wallet tab → Withdraw Funds):
- Paid via **Wise** to your email — works with any Philippine bank, GCash, or even kept as PHP balance on Wise.
- **No minimum withdrawal.** Withdraw any positive amount you have.
- **You receive the full requested amount.** Wise transfer fees are paid by Tendso — not deducted from your payout.
- First-time payouts: if you don't have a Wise account, the email becomes a 7-day claim invitation. Sign up free at wise.com (or use the referral link in the app for a bonus).

## 7. The submission process — step by step

Once the owner says yes, this is what you do:

### Step 1 — Business info (2 min)
Fill in: business name, type (food / retail / services / etc.), owner name, owner phone, owner email (optional but useful), full address, city.

### Step 2 — Photos (3 min)
Upload **3 to 10 photos.** Required shot list per the training:
1. **Portrait** — owner from the chest up, eye-level, no obstructions.
2. **Location** — the storefront, signage, or workspace from outside.
3. **Craft / product** — at least one shot of what they make or sell.

Lighting matters. Face the light, avoid backlighting. Golden hour (early morning, late afternoon) is best.

### Step 3 — Interview (3–5 min)
Either:
- **Video** (₱500 payout) — record landscape, hold steady, mic close.
- **Audio** (₱300 payout) — quiet spot, phone close to the owner's mouth, kill background noise.

**Interview tips** (from in-app training):
1. Introduce yourself and the business (warm-up).
2. Ask the **origin story** — how did this business start?
3. Ask what makes their business **special** (product quality, family recipe, hand-made, location, service).
4. Ask about **products / services** offered.
5. Ask about **operating hours and contact info**.

Speak slowly, give them time. The AI transcribes and extracts the answers — clear speech matters more than fancy questions.

### Step 4 — Review (1 min)
Confirm package (Standard / With Custom Domain). If custom domain, type the domain — the app checks availability against Hostinger live. If unavailable, the app suggests alternatives. Confirm "I confirm this is a real business" and submit.

### Step 5 — Wait (24–48 hours)
- Our team reviews + the AI generates a draft website.
- The business owner gets an email with a payment link (₱1,000 or ₱1,500 to GCash, Maya, or bank).
- Once they pay, the website goes live + you get paid.

## 8. Common objections + answers

### *"₱1,000 is a lot."*

> *"Po, isang bayad lang yan — hindi monthly. Ibig sabihin sa isang taon, less than ₱100 a month yan para sa professional na website. Yung Facebook page niyo, libre lang, pero hindi kayo nakikita sa Google search. Yan ang difference."*

If they still hesitate: offer Standard tier (₱1,000), not the custom domain (₱1,500). Don't oversell.

### *"May Facebook page na ako."*

> *"Maganda po yan, pero yung Facebook page niyo, nasa loob ng Facebook lang siya. Yung website po, kapag sinearch sa Google ang business niyo, lalabas — kahit walang Facebook account yung customer. Plus, kayo ang may-ari ng website — hindi mawawala kahit suspended ang Facebook."*

### *"Hindi ako marunong sa tech."*

> *"Wag po kayo mag-alala — wala po kayong gagawin pagkatapos ng interview. Ako po ang mag-uupload ng lahat. Ang inyo lang po, bayaran yung service fee tapos i-share yung website link sa mga customers niyo."*

### *"Paano kung wala akong gusto sa output?"*

> *"Bago bayaran, makikita po ninyo yung draft. Kung may baguhin, sabihin lang sa amin within 7 days. Pagka-approve niyo, saka pa lang bayaran."*

(In-platform: drafts have an "Edit" cycle before payment. After payment, edits are minor only — set expectations honestly.)

### *"Bakit ₱1,500 yung may custom domain? Saan napupunta?"*

> *"Yung ₱500 dagdag, yan ang ginagamit pang-bayad sa domain niyo for year 1. Around ₱1,120 (~$20) talaga ang yearly cost ng domain — kami po ang nagbabayad ng first year. Kayo ang may-ari ng domain, hindi kami. Kapag gusto niyong i-renew next year, kayo na po, around ₱1,120 lang ulit."*

### *"What if it doesn't work out?"*

> *"Wala kayong babayaran kung hindi pa live yung website. Ang flow po: ako interview → team gumagawa → kayo nakikita → kayo bayad → live na po."*

The fee is collected only after the website is built and reviewed. No money up front.

### *"Yung kapitbahay ko, ₱500 lang sinisingil niya."*

There's always someone cheaper. What we offer for ₱1,000 includes:
- Custom AI-generated content (not a template they fill in)
- Professional photos enhanced via AI
- Hosting + SSL + 24/7 uptime
- Content edits within 7 days post-launch
- Optional custom domain for ₱500 more

Be honest if a competitor is genuinely cheaper for their use case. Don't fight on price alone — fight on what's included and how stable the result is.

## 9. Quality bar — what gets approved fast vs. what gets sent back

Submissions are reviewed by our team. **Approved fast** = paid faster.

### Green-light submissions look like:
- Photos: well-lit, in focus, business is clearly identifiable.
- Interview: audible speech, owner answers questions clearly, 1–3 minutes.
- Business info: complete address, real phone number, real email if collected.
- Domain (if custom): an available, reasonable name that matches the business.

### Yellow flags (might be sent back for re-do):
- Photos with motion blur or extreme backlighting.
- Audio with heavy background noise (jeepney passing, loud TV, kids screaming).
- Interview that's just "hi, this is my business, salamat po" (we need real story).
- Business name in interview doesn't match the business info form.

### Red flags (rejected — don't submit these):
- Fake / made-up businesses.
- Businesses you didn't actually visit (drive-by photos from outside).
- Owner didn't agree (yes, we can tell — owners we follow up with by email).
- Profanity or inappropriate content.

**Don't sacrifice your approval rate for volume.** One good submission a day pays better than three rushed ones that get rejected.

## 11. FAQ — quick answers to the most-asked questions

**Q: How long until my first earnings?**
A: 24–48 hours for our review, then 1–7 days for the business owner to pay. Average creator-to-payout: under a week per submission.

**Q: Can I submit a business I already have a relationship with?**
A: Yes — that's the easiest first submission. Family business, friend's tindahan, your parents' barber. Faster yes, faster payment.

**Q: How many businesses can I submit per day?**
A: No hard cap, but quality matters more than volume (see §9). Most active creators submit 1–3/week.

**Q: What if the business owner asks if it's legit?**
A: Show your **Certificate of Completion** in the Profile tab (proof you're a Certified Creator with Tendso). Direct them to a live example site.

**Q: Does the website have to be in English?**
A: No. The AI handles Filipino and Taglish. The interview can be in any language the owner is comfortable in — the AI transcribes and the resulting site uses the same language tone.

**Q: What if the owner wants edits after launch?**
A: Within 7 days post-launch, edits are free. After that, they can request changes via support.

**Q: Can the owner cancel after payment?**
A: Refunds within 7 days if the website hasn't gone live yet. After live, no refunds — the work is done. Be honest about this when pitching.

**Q: Do I have to be Filipino to be a creator?**
A: No, but the businesses are. You need to be in the Philippines and able to do interviews in Tagalog or any major regional language.
